# Welcome to Julien's Learning Vault

This Learning Vault is a digital knowledge-management system based on the Zettelkasten ("slip box") method, a note-taking and learning approach developed by German sociologist Niklas Luhmann. Rather than organizing information into isolated subjects or assignments, a Zettelkasten is designed to build a network of interconnected ideas. New knowledge is linked to existing knowledge, creating a web of relationships that grows over time.

This vault is a record of my learning journey, showcasing my natural learning style which is associative. Source materials, including books, articles, lectures, videos, and online courses, are connected to written reflections, research notes, essays, and original insights. The resulting network provides a transparent record of how ideas were explored, understood, and integrated across multiple disciplines.

For many autistic learners who naturally learn through pattern recognition, connection-making, and interdisciplinary thinking, understanding develops through relationships between concepts rather than through isolated memorization of facts. This vault was designed to support that style of learning by documenting not only what was learned, but also how new ideas connect to existing knowledge.

The graph view provides a visual representation of these connections. Source materials are linked to written work, and related concepts are connected across subjects such as ecology, biology, anthropology, ethics, environmental science, and human behavior. This creates a living portfolio that demonstrates research, critical thinking, written communication, knowledge synthesis, and long-term intellectual development.
