# Veterinary medicine (1)

This lecture explores the diverse roles and evolution of veterinary medicine, highlighting its connection to societal views on animals.

Veterinary Medicine Specializations and Roles

- Veterinarians work in various fields including small animal care, food supply, equine care, zoos, research, shelter medicine, and wildlife rehabilitation.
- Specializations within veterinary medicine mirror human medicine, covering areas like anesthesia, dermatology, cardiology, and oncology.

Historical and Gender Dynamics in Veterinary Medicine

- Early veterinary practice was informal, male-dominated, and involved harsh treatments; women faced significant barriers to entry and training.
- Gender discrimination in admissions persisted until the 1970s, after which the profession saw feminization, with women now constituting about 80% of veterinary students.

Changing Human-Animal Relationships and Veterinary Practice

- Increasingly, pets are viewed as family members, leading to growth in companion animal healthcare.
- Women are more represented in companion animal practice compared to food animal care, reflecting shifts in societal values and the human-animal bond.
