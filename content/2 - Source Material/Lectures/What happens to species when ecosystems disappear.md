# What happens to species when ecosystems disappear

This lecture focuses on the ecological importance of lemurs in Madagascar's tropical rainforests and the consequences of their decline.

Lemurs and Madagascar's Biodiversity

- Madagascar is a biodiversity hotspot with many unique species, including 103-111 lemur species.
- Some lemur species have gone extinct, possibly due to climate change, habitat loss, and human arrival.

Causes of Lemur Decline

- Primary threats include habitat loss from slash-and-burn agriculture and deforestation for charcoal.
- Historical factors like French colonial deforestation and invasive species introduction have worsened the crisis.

Ecological Role and Research on Lemurs

- Lemurs are key seed dispersers, essential for forest regeneration and biodiversity maintenance.
- Research involves observing lemur behavior, seed dispersal experiments, and modeling forest changes without lemurs.

The study of lemurs in Madagascar provides insights into the broader impacts of losing key species in tropical forests worldwide, highlighting the importance of conserving these ecosystems.
