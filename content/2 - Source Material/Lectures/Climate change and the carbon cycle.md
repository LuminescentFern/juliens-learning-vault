# Climate change and the carbon cycle

The lecture explains how human activities are impacting the Earth's atmosphere and climate, using data from the Mauna Loa Observatory as a key example.

Carbon Dioxide Seasonal Patterns

- Carbon dioxide levels show a regular seasonal cycle, decreasing in spring and summer due to plant photosynthesis absorbing CO2.
- In fall and winter, plant decay releases CO2 back into the atmosphere, causing levels to rise again.

Long-Term Increase in CO2

- Over recent decades, atmospheric CO2 levels have steadily increased, largely due to human activities.
- Deforestation and burning fossil fuels release carbon stored in plants and underground reservoirs into the atmosphere.

Climate Change and Its Effects

- CO2 acts as a greenhouse gas, trapping heat and causing global warming.
- This warming leads to melting polar ice, rising sea levels, and more severe natural disasters like hurricanes and floods, exemplified by Hurricane Harvey's impact in Houston.
