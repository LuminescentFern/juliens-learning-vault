# Biophilia and cultural considerations for biodiversity loss

This course content explains the importance of biodiversity and the deep connections humans have with living things beyond just ecological functions.

The importance of biodiversity

- Loss of biodiversity threatens natural ecosystems and the ecosystem services they provide to humans.
- Species disappearance results in losing essential ecological services and emotional, cultural, and spiritual connections.

Human affinity for nature

- Interacting with living things improves mental health and well-being, a concept called biophilia by biologist Edward O. Wilson.
- Animals and plants have symbolic, cultural, and spiritual significance in many societies worldwide.

Cultural and emotional connections

- Stories, art, and traditions often feature animals and plants, reflecting human traits and values.
- The loss of species means losing these cultural symbols and spiritual connections that have been part of human experience for millennia.
