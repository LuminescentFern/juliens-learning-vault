# How biodiversity affects people - disease

This lecture explains the emergence and impact of zoonotic diseases, focusing on how diseases like AIDS originated and spread from animals to humans.

Origins of AIDS and HIV

- AIDS appeared in the early 1980s in the US, caused by the HIV virus that attacks the human immune system.
- HIV originated from primate viruses (SIV) in Central Africa, likely transmitted to humans through contact with chimpanzees.

Zoonotic Diseases and Their Spread

- Zoonotic diseases, which come from animals, make up about 60% of infectious diseases, including Ebola, influenza, and COVID-19.
- Human factors such as large populations, social behavior, and global travel facilitate the rapid spread of these diseases.

Human Interaction with Wildlife

- Many new zoonotic diseases come from wild animals due to habitat destruction, hunting, and wildlife trade.
- Closer contact between humans and wild animals increases the risk of disease transmission.

Conservation and Public Health

- Protecting natural habitats and reducing human encroachment can help prevent future zoonotic outbreaks.
- Conservation biology is increasingly linked with medicine and public health to safeguard both wildlife and human health.
