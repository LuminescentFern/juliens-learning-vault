# Changes in population size

This lecture explains how population size changes over time and how to estimate it using the mark-recapture technique, along with mathematical modeling of population growth.

Mark-Recapture and Population Sampling

- The mark-recapture technique estimates population size by sampling the same population twice and counting individuals captured both times.
- Timing between samples matters: too short may capture the same individuals repeatedly; too long may allow population size to change due to births, deaths, immigration, or emigration.

Factors Affecting Population Size

- Population size increases through births and immigration.
- Population size decreases through deaths and emigration.
- The balance of these four processes determines whether a population grows or shrinks.

Mathematical Modeling of Population Growth

- Population growth rate is modeled by the differential equation dN/dt = rN, where r is the intrinsic rate of natural increase and N is population size.
- When population size is small, growth is slow but accelerates as N increases.
- Growth eventually slows and levels off at the carrying capacity (K), modeled by modifying the equation to include the term (K-N)/K, which reduces growth as N approaches K.
