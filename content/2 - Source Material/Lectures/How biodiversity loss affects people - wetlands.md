# How biodiversity loss affects people - wetlands

The course content explains the importance of wetlands and their role in supporting biodiversity and human well-being.

Wetlands and Ecosystem Services

- Wetlands act as natural filters, removing harmful pollutants like metals and excess nitrogen from the environment.
- They are highly productive ecosystems that convert sunlight into chemical energy, supporting various organisms.

Wetlands as Habitat and Flood Control

- Many fish and shellfish consumed by people depend on wetlands for part or all of their life cycles.
- Wetlands provide natural flood control by absorbing large volumes of water, reducing flood risks in populated areas.

Coastal Wetlands and Mangroves

- Coastal wetlands, such as mangroves, are under threat from human activity and rising sea levels.
- Mangroves stabilize shorelines, serve as nurseries for seafood species, trap sediments protecting coral reefs, and shield coastlines from storm surges, benefiting millions of people.
