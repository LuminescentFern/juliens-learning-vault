# Pollination and our food supply


This course content explains the essential role of ecosystem services, focusing on pollination and its importance to both natural ecosystems and human agriculture.

Ecosystem Services and Pollination

- Ecosystem services are vital roles played by living organisms that support life, such as pollination.
- Pollination involves transferring pollen from the male to the female parts of flowers, enabling plant reproduction.

Pollinators and Their Importance

- Many plants rely on animal pollinators like insects, especially bees, for efficient fertilization.
- About 80% of crop plants worldwide depend on insect pollinators, including fruits, nuts, coffee, and plants used for animal feed.

Threats to Pollinators

- Pollinators face threats such as pesticide use (neonicotinoids), parasitic mites, disease spread from hive movement, and habitat loss due to urbanization and agriculture.
- Declines in pollinator populations, including honeybees and wild bees, pose risks to food production and biodiversity.
