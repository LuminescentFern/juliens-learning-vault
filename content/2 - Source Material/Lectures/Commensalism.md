# Commensalism

This course content explains different types of ecological interactions between species, focusing on how species affect each other in their environments.

Types of Species Interactions

- Commensalism: One species benefits while the other is neither helped nor harmed, exemplified by cattle egrets feeding on insects stirred up by cattle without affecting the cattle.
- Competition and Predation: Both species are negatively impacted in competition, while in predation or parasitism, one benefits and the other is harmed.

Examples and Dynamics

- Oxpeckers feed on parasites of zebras, benefiting both species in a mutualistic relationship.
- However, oxpeckers can sometimes harm zebras by pecking them for blood, showing how interactions can shift from mutualism to parasitism.

Ecological Significance

- These interactions illustrate the complex and dynamic relationships in ecosystems.
- Understanding these relationships is key to studying ecology and how species coexist and impact each other.
