# The sixth mass extinction

This lecture discusses the history and causes of mass extinction events on Earth, emphasizing the current human-driven extinction crisis and conservation efforts.

Mass Extinction Events

- There have been five major mass extinction events in Earth's history, with the most severe at the end of the Permian period wiping out up to 96% of species.
- The most recent mass extinction, 66 million years ago, caused the dinosaurs' extinction, likely due to an asteroid impact, while causes of earlier events remain debated.

Human Impact on Extinctions

- Humans have caused many extinctions since arriving in new regions, often through hunting large animals and habitat modification.
- The current extinction rate is about 35 times higher than the natural background rate, comparable only to past mass extinctions.

Conservation Examples

- The Houston toad and whooping crane are examples of endangered species threatened by habitat loss and human activity.
- Conservation efforts like captive breeding, habitat protection, and careful management have helped prevent their extinction, highlighting the importance of human intervention to avoid a sixth mass extinction.
