# Amazon forest fragmentation experiment

This course content explains the impact of habitat loss and fragmentation on species, using the Amazon rainforest as a key example.

Habitat Fragmentation and Island Biogeography

- The equilibrium theory of island biogeography predicts that smaller and more isolated habitats support fewer species.
- Habitat fragmentation creates isolated patches that function like islands, affecting species diversity and community structure.

The Biological Dynamics of Forest Fragmentation Project (BDFFP)

- The BDFFP in Northern Brazil experimentally isolated forest patches of different sizes to study fragmentation effects.
- Isolated patches lost species over time, especially large predators and group-living animals, due to limited space and resources.

Ecological Consequences of Fragmentation

- Birds and insects dependent on specific ecological interactions also declined in isolated patches.
- Edge effects caused increased tree mortality and changes in tree species composition, reducing habitat quality.

Broader Implications

- Even protected reserves cannot fully maintain intact forest communities if surrounded by cleared land.
- The findings from the Amazon highlight the global threat habitat fragmentation poses to biodiversity.
