# Veterinary medicine (2)

This course content focuses on the complexities and conflicts in veterinary medicine, especially in companion animal practice, arising from the changing societal views on animals.

Development and Context of Veterinary Medicine

- Veterinary medicine has grown in size and sophistication due to increased pet ownership and the elevated status of animals as family members.
- The veterinarian-client-animal relationship is a three-way interaction where the client owns the animal but the veterinarian is responsible for the animal's welfare.

Sources of Conflict in Veterinary Care

- Conflicts arise from differing attitudes toward animals, such as varying willingness to spend on treatment or decisions about euthanasia.
- Disagreements also stem from differing beliefs about standards of care and quality of life for animals, with clients and veterinarians sometimes having opposing views.

Veterinarian's Role and Ethical Challenges

- Animals are legally considered property, complicating the veterinarian's role as an advocate for the animal.
- Veterinarians face tension between advocating for the animal's best interests and respecting the client's decisions and financial constraints.
- This dual role creates challenging social interactions during veterinary-client encounters.
