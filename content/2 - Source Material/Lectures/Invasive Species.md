# Invasive Species

This course content explains how humans impact the natural world by moving organisms to new places, sometimes intentionally, which can lead to ecological consequences.

Intentional Introduction of Species

- Humans introduce plants and animals to new areas for aesthetic, agricultural, or cultural reasons, such as European settlers bringing livestock to the Americas.
- An example is the introduction of European starlings to New York City in 1890, which has led to a massive population increase and significant agricultural damage.

Invasive Species and Their Effects

- Species that thrive and cause harm in new environments are called invasive species.
- The brown tree snake, introduced accidentally to Guam, caused the extinction of native birds by preying on them, demonstrating the severe impact invasive species can have.

Ecological Consequences of Species Introduction

- The loss of native birds on Guam led to an explosion in spider populations and disrupted seed dispersal for plants, threatening forest diversity.
- Overall, the spread of organisms by humans is reducing global biological diversity and altering ecosystems.
