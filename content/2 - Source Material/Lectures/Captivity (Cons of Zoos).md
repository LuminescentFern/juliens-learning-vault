# Captivity (Cons of Zoos)

This lecture explores the complex issues surrounding zoos, focusing on the ethical and practical implications of keeping animals in captivity and whether zoos fulfill their stated purposes.

Ethical and Physical Impact of Captivity

- Captivity involves a power imbalance where animals depend on humans, often leading to physical and psychological harm.
- Many species, especially intelligent ones like elephants and great apes, suffer from restricted movement, stress behaviors, weakened immune systems, and failure to thrive.

Educational and Scientific Claims of Zoos

- Zoos claim to educate the public and inspire conservation, but research shows visitors spend little time engaging with educational materials or animals.
- Scientific research on captive animals is controversial because their behavior in captivity differs from the wild, limiting the applicability of findings.

Conservation and Societal Considerations

- Captive breeding programs have saved some species but raise concerns about genetic diversity and the fate of surplus animals.
- The existence of zoos raises questions about human rights to observe wild animals and challenges society to find compassionate alternatives to captivity.
