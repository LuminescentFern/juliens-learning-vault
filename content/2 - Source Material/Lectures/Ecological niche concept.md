# Ecological niche concept

This course content explains the concept of ecological niches through the study of barnacle species in the rocky intertidal zone, highlighting how both abiotic and biotic factors influence species distribution and resource use.

Ecological Niche Definitions

- Joseph Grinnell defined niche as the habitat and adaptations of a species.
- Charles Elton expanded this to include all roles a species plays and the resources it uses in its environment.

Hutchinson's Niche Visualization

- G. Evelyn Hutchinson proposed representing niches graphically with resource use on axes.
- The niche often forms a curve showing potential versus actual resource use, called fundamental and realized niches.

Barnacle Niche Example

- Experiments showed balanus barnacles have a wide potential niche but a narrower actual niche due to competition.
- Semibalanus barnacles have similar potential and actual niches, limited to a narrower range.

Key Ecological Insights

- A species' actual niche is always within its potential niche.
- Overlapping potential niches can lead to reduced overlap in actual niches, influencing community structure.
