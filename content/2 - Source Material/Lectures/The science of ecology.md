# The science of ecology

The course content introduces the field of ecology, focusing on understanding how living organisms interact with each other and their physical environment across different scales.

Foundations of Ecology

- Alexander Von Humboldt's early 19th-century observations highlighted the interconnectedness of living things and their environments, laying the groundwork for ecology.
- Ecology aims to document and understand interactions among organisms and between organisms and non-living environmental factors.

Levels of Ecological Study

- Organism ecology examines individual species traits and behaviours, such as a flower's structure and reproductive timing.
- Population ecology studies groups of the same species, including population size, growth, and habitat changes.
- Community ecology explores interactions among different species, such as pollination and competition.
- Ecosystem ecology considers both living organisms and non-living components like soil, water, and sunlight.
- Landscape ecology looks at how different ecosystems interact and how organisms and resources move across them.
- Global ecology studies the interconnectedness of all ecosystems on Earth.

Purpose and Application of Ecology

- Ecology satisfies a natural curiosity about how the natural world works, often sparked by childhood wonder.
- Ecological knowledge helps understand human impacts on nature and supports efforts to protect ecosystems and promote healthier human lives
