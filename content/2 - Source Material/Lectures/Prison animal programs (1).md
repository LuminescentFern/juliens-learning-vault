# Prison animal programs (1)

This course item discusses prison animal programs and their role in benefiting both inmates and animals, highlighting their rehabilitative potential.

Overview of Prison Animal Programs

- These programs began officially in the U.S. in the 1980s and involve inmates working with animals such as dogs, cats, horses, and wildlife.
- Activities include training dogs for adoption or assistance, grooming services, taming wild horses, wildlife rehabilitation, and raising animals for food production.

Goals and Benefits

- The primary goal is inmate rehabilitation, aiming to reduce recidivism by providing skills, confidence, and a sense of responsibility.
- Programs also serve community needs, such as providing trained assistance dogs or rehabilitated animals for adoption.

Criticisms and Challenges

- Critics argue that prison should focus on punishment rather than enjoyable activities like animal care.
- Concerns include limited job opportunities for former inmates using these skills, low inmate wages undercutting markets, and exclusion of some inmates from participation.
- The diversity of program designs makes it difficult to measure overall effectiveness or compare outcomes across programs.
