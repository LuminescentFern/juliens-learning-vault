# How to write a mini-essay

- Only focus on one idea per mini-essay
- Link between ideas/notes
- Keep them 100-500 words long
- 10-20 minutes of writing
- Keep it short enough that you can see everything on your computer screen without scrolling
- Stay organized with tags and references
- Link related mini-essays
  
  # 3 Part Structure:

- Intro: The hook. This is where you gain the attention of your reader and present the core idea (or premise) of the mini essay. It doesn't need to be long. A sentence or two is good enough. Present the premise with as much clarity as possible.
  
  Strategies -  Spark curiosity by teasing with hidden details. Inspire emotion. Ask a question. Tell a story.
  
- Body: Write just enough to answer the question and get your point across, but not so much that you're rambling. 

Strategies - Look away from the screen for 10 seconds and look back. Read it as if you had never read it before. This will allow you to notice mistakes.
  
- Conclusion: Summarize and make an impact. Use a sentence or two to pin the main idea down. The conclusion doesn't need to be long.
