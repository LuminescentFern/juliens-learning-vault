# Animals and institutions

This course content introduces the concept of institutions from a sociological perspective, focusing on how animals are involved within these institutions.

Definition and Scope of Institutions

- Institutions can refer to organizations, facilities, or established customs and practices recognized collectively by society.
- Sociologists apply the term to diverse phenomena such as family, religion, economy, education, and even specific practices like handshakes.

Institutions as Collectively Recognized Beliefs and Practices

- Institutions consist of shared beliefs and standardized ways of doing things that shape people's actions, opportunities, and socialization.
- Despite their variety, all institutions have this common feature of collective recognition and influence.

Animals within Institutional Contexts

- Laboratory science is an institution with specific methods, including the use of animal models in research.
- Other institutions involving animals include agriculture (livestock and meat production), sport and entertainment (e.g., dog fighting, zoos), and animal health and welfare (veterinary medicine and shelters).
