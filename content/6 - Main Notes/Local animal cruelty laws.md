# Local animal cruelty laws

Find out about the laws that currently exist against animal cruelty that exist near you. Pay particular attention to how they define cruelty and what exceptions they make.

----------------- 

In Newfoundland, Canada, The Animal cruelty laws are focused on distress. The laws basically define distress as not getting proper food, water, shelter, or care, being sick, injured, abused, or in pain, experiencing neglect, being kept in bad conditions, being unprotected from extreme heat or cold weather. If you own or have custody of an animal, you are legally responsible for it. However, there are exceptions such as agriculture, hunting, fishing, euthanasia, and animal used for work or services. Along with this, Wildlife is handled under separate laws and the act mostly focuses on domestic animals. The law protects animals only up to a certain point, mainly protecting companion animals, and it is mostly about not allowing unjustified suffering.
