# Reflections on species interaction

Serengeti National Park in Tanzania is a place of high mammal diversity. Two of the most common species in this ecosystem are Wildebeest and Zebra, which migrate through the park each year, with numbers in the millions.

Describe three ways in which these two species might be interacting with each other. In addition, name three other species/organisms that Zebra and Wildebeest might be interacting with in the park, and describe their potential interaction.

------

Both Zebra and Wildebeest have similar but different diets, which allows for mutual feeding where both benefit from one another. Zebras will eat the taller, tougher grass which allows for the wildebeest to get to their preferred shorter grass beneath. The two species still both need water however, and that's how competition rises. Water is scarce for them and if they find any they may compete for it. While the two species travel in such large herds, zebras good eyesight and wildebeests strong smell and hearing make it much easier for them to detect predators, making it beneficial for them to travel together. The zebra and wildebeest may also encounter lions and hyenas that hunt them, and dung beetles that collect the zebra and wildebeests waste.
