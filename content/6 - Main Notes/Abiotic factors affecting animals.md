# Abiotic factors affecting animals

Let's pause for a moment and think about an animal living in your area. What are some abiotic factors that might affect this animal, eg. a butterfly? In what way?

(Please briefly describe your environment and the current season when you put in your answer.)

---- 

Every summer my area gets wildfires, which can kill moose and destroy their habitats. During summers we can also have dry spells. These cause plants to dry up which can affect their food supply. In the winters we get a lot of snow which can bury ground forage and make moving around exhausting for them. As we approach summer, there has been rain and sudden bursts of heat, soon summer will fully set in, causing dry spells and consequently wildfires. Despite this, moose are highly resilient animals and have adapted ways to survive the weather, such as eating twigs and the bark of woody plants that sit above the snowline.
