# Invasive species in my region

Different locations in the world often deal with different types of invasive species.

- What types of invasive species are prominent in your region? If you don't know of any off the top of your head, you can search the internet.
    
- What do these invasive species do that is problematic for the ecosystem?
    
- What actions are being taken in your region to mitigate the impact of these species?

---------

It was recently announced that Japanese beetles have been discovered in the St.John's region. This beetle is an invasive species, and a severe threat to Newfoundland ecosystems. These beetles stem from Japan and parts of Northeastern Asia where they are kept in check by natural predators, however, they have no natural predators in Newfoundland. 

Japanese beetles attack over 300 plant species. Crops, wildberries and turf are particularly susceptible as they target both the root (larvae stage) and above ground fruit and foliage (adult beetle). They are so thorough that they can completely wipe out a garden bed within 24-48 hours, leaving nothing but skeletal remains. Annual vegetation is completely destroyed, such as strawberries and raspberries, and trees and shrubs are weakened. 

The government is taking this threat seriously, and has begun using larvicides in the area where the beetles have been located, as well as creating a quarantine, meaning people are not allowed to remove gardening supplies and soil from the area of concern. 

Although the government may sound proactive, similar approaches in neighbouring provinces such as Nova Scotia failed.
