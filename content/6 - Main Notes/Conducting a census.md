# Conducting a census

In this lesson, we discussed some typical ways to conduct population censuses. But there are lots of different ways to study populations! For this activity, you will conduct your own population census. This can be in a yard, a park, on the street, looking out a window, or in your own mind.

To do this:

1. Pick an organism that you will census. This can be grass, a species of bird, ants, whatever you can find.
    
2. Select your survey method. Are you going to count individuals? Are you going to use sampling plots?
    
3. Select your survey size. How many places are you going to look for your species? How big will the area be? How many times are you going to look for the species?
    
4. Go out and do the sampling! (Or imagine it)
    
5. Reflect. How did your census go? Was it easy? Difficult? How many individuals of your organism did you see? Was this representative of the whole population? How might you go about calculating the size of the population?

------

I chose snails and the mark-recapture method. I went to a small patch of forested area (roughly 50 meters by 50 meters) shortly after rain and marked 80 snails with a white dot on their shell, then I waited until it rained again (2 days later.) I came back and thoroughly searched until I had found 60 snails, 15 of them had the white dots where as 45 did not. 25% of the snails found were marked but there were 80 marked snails, and after doing the math there were roughly 0.13 snails per square meter.
