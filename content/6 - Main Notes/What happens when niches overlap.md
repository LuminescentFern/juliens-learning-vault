# What happens when niches overlap

The course content explains how the ecological niche concept helps ecologists understand species' resource use and competition outcomes.

Competition and Niche Overlap

- When species use the same resources, their ecological niches overlap, leading to competition.
- Joseph Connell's barnacle studies showed competitive displacement, where one species outcompetes and excludes another from a habitat.

Examples of Competitive Displacement

- Arctic foxes could live further south but are limited by competition with red foxes for den sites.
- Red foxes outcompete arctic foxes in overlapping regions, restricting arctic fox distribution.

Resource Partitioning as a Competition Outcome

- Species may adjust resource use to reduce competition, a process called resource partitioning.
- In Houston, native Carolina anoles shift to higher vertical habitats when invasive brown anoles are present, reducing direct competition.
