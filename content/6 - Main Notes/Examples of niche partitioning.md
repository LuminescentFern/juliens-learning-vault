# Examples of niche partitioning

We've seen a few examples of species that share niches and how they are able to both survive while using similar resources (foxes).

What is an example of two species with very similar niches that you can think of, and what do you know about how they are both able to survive? Maybe even from your own backyard. Share a picture of the two species if you can find one.

-----

Seagulls and crows both scavenge for food, and I've often seen them fight over it. Crows survive the competition from seagulls because of their intelligence. Although the seagull population is huge and resource draining, crows have the intelligence to find food even when its scarce. Seagulls survive the competition from crows because of how much they are willing to eat. Seagulls have been found with their stomach filled with plastic and trash, and although this is by no means healthy, eating whatever they can find has been what's caused their population numbers to be so high. They choose what makes food and survival the easiest. 

Crow example: Crows can be seen placing walnuts in traffic to crack them open. 

Seagull example: Seagulls will wait outside fast food places for food to be dropped, they can be seen swarming any dropped food.
