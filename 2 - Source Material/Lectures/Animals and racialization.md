# Animals and racialization


This course content explains how animals and animal-related practices are used to racialize people and groups, assigning racial meanings that justify exclusion or mistreatment.

Racialization and Animals

- Racialization is the process by which something gains racial meaning in society, often leading to discrimination.
- Animals and animal practices can be racialized, linking certain behaviors with specific racial or ethnic groups.

Animal Practices and Racialization

- Animal practices include activities like walking dogs, eating animal products, and events like dog fighting or rodeos.
- When these practices are associated with minority groups, they can be portrayed as illegitimate or immoral compared to similar practices by dominant groups.

Examples of Racialization through Animals

- People of color have historically been compared to animals to dehumanize them, such as political cartoons depicting Barack Obama as a chimpanzee.
- The Mexican charreada (rodeo) event horse tripping is criminalized in some U.S. states, while similar Anglo American rodeo practices are exempt, illustrating racial double standards.
- Other examples include live animal markets in Chinatown, Caribbean animal sacrifices, and Filipino dog-eating practices, all targeted with double standards compared to majority group practices.
