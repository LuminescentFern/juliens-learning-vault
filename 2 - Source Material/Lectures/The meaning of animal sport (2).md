# The meaning of animal sport (2)

This lecture explores the connections between sex, masculinity, aggression, and animal sports, highlighting how these elements shape the cultural and social meanings of such sports.

Masculinity and Sport

- Sports historically have been male-created and male-dominated spaces, serving as arenas to cultivate and demonstrate masculinity.
- Masculinity is seen as requiring continual validation through traits like aggression, toughness, risk-taking, physical strength, sexual virility, and competition.

Animal Sports as Masculine Rituals

- Blood sports like dog fighting, cockfighting, and bullfighting symbolize masculine identity, with animals standing in for men in displays of aggression and dominance.
- In these sports, animals represent their owners' social status and character, reinforcing aggressive masculinity through rituals and male bonding.

Equestrian Sports and Masculine Culture

- Even in equestrian sports where men and women compete equally, masculine norms of control and domination prevail, rooted in military riding traditions.
- Horses are trained through coercive methods, symbolizing human dominance over animals, and the rider-horse relationship reflects masculine ideology of control and teamwork.
