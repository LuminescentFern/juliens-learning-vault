# Animals in laboratories (3)

This lecture explores the complex status and roles of mice in biomedical research, highlighting how they transition from animals to scientific tools.

Roles and Perceptions of Mice

- Mice can be pets with individual identities or pests subject to inhumane killing methods without mourning.
- Feeder mice raise ethical dilemmas as they serve as live food for carnivorous pets, balancing the interests of two animals.

Development of Laboratory Mice

- The white albino mouse became the standard research subject due to its reproductive capacity and genetic stability through inbreeding.
- Scientists breed specialized strains, including knockout and transgenic mice, to study genetics and diseases with controlled variables.

Ethical and Legal Considerations

- Laboratory mice are transformed into data sources, losing their status as individual animals and are excluded from certain animal welfare regulations.
- The Three Rs principle guides research to refine methods, replace animals when possible, and reduce numbers, but increasing demand in biotechnology challenges these goals.
