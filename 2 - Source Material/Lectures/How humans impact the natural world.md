# How humans impact the natural world

This lecture discusses the transformation of Rice University's campus and its surrounding environment, highlighting the impact of human activity on natural habitats and the importance of conservation biology.

Rice University Campus and Ecosystems

- Originally, the campus was on the outskirts of Houston, surrounded by diverse ecosystems including prairies, wetlands, and forests.
- Today, the campus is an urban green oasis with over 4,000 trees and more than 100 species, serving as habitat for over 255 bird species, especially during migration.

Habitat Restoration and Conservation Efforts

- The Harris Gully natural area on campus is being restored to its original coastal prairie ecosystem using native plants.
- This restoration serves both as a wildlife refuge and a research site to study biodiversity changes due to habitat restoration.

Human Impact and Habitat Loss

- Human activities have drastically altered about three-quarters of terrestrial and two-thirds of marine ecosystems worldwide.
- Habitat loss occurs through land conversion to urban, suburban, and agricultural uses, as well as habitat degradation like pollution.

Conservation Biology and Its Goals

- Conservation biologists study ecology at multiple levels to understand and predict the effects of habitat loss.
- Their work aims to inform strategies to preserve biodiversity and ensure species survival in the future despite ongoing environmental changes.
