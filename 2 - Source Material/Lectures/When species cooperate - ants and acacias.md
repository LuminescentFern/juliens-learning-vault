# When species cooperate - ants and acacias

This course content explains a mutualistic relationship between the bullshorn acacia tree and Pseudomyrmex ants in Central America.

Mutualism between Bullshorn Acacia and Ants

- The bullshorn acacia has hollow thorns that serve as living quarters for a colony of Pseudomyrmex ants.
- The ants live inside the thorns, which act like apartments in a high-rise building, all belonging to the same colony.

Benefits to the Ants

- The ants gain shelter and protection by living inside the hollow thorns of the acacia tree.
- The tree also provides food for the ants in the form of extra floral nectar and protein-rich Beltian bodies on its leaflets.

Benefits to the Acacia Tree

- The ants defend the tree aggressively against herbivores like caterpillars and grasshoppers that try to eat its leaves.
- This defense acts as a standing army protecting the tree, making the relationship mutually beneficial, known as mutualism in ecology.
