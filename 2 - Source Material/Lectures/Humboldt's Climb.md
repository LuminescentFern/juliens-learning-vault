# Humboldt's Climb

This course item describes Alexander von Humboldt's 1802 expedition to climb Mt. Chimborazo in Ecuador, which led to foundational insights in ecology.

Humboldt's Expedition and Observations

- Humboldt and his team carried scientific instruments to measure temperature, air pressure, humidity, and air composition as they climbed Chimborazo.
- They observed how plant species changed with elevation, noting similarities between high-altitude plants near the equator and those in northern Europe and the Arctic.

Key Ecological Insights

- Humboldt realized that moving up in altitude is similar to moving up in latitude in terms of environmental conditions.
- He proposed that changes in physical factors like temperature and air pressure drive the distribution of plant species.

Legacy and Impact on Ecology

- Although they did not reach the summit due to altitude sickness and obstacles, Humboldt's detailed notes and sketches laid the groundwork for ecology.
- He emphasized the interconnectedness of living organisms and their environments, establishing ecology as a science focused on these relationships.
