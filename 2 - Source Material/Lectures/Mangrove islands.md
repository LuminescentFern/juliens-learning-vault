# Mangrove islands

The course content explains an experiment testing MacArthur and Wilson's equilibrium theory of island biogeography, which predicts species diversity on islands based on island size and isolation.

Experiment Setup and Location

- Wilson and Simberloff chose mangrove islands in Florida Bay, which vary in size and are suitable for tracking species.
- They cleared the islands of all species using tents and pesticide gas to start with a clean slate.

Observations and Results

- They monitored recolonization by insects and other invertebrates over a year.
- Larger islands were recolonized faster and supported more species; more isolated islands took longer and had fewer species.

Confirmation of Theory

- After about a year, species numbers stabilized, matching initial observations before clearing.
- This equilibrium supported MacArthur and Wilson's theory that island species diversity is predictable by island size and isolation.
