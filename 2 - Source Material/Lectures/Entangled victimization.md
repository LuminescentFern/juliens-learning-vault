# Entangled victimization

This lecture explains the concept of entangled victimization, where children become coerced into the abuse of their pets as a form of control and trauma.

Definition and Context of Entangled Victimization

- Entangled victimization involves children being forced to witness or participate in the abuse of their pets by adults, distinct from direct victimization or children initiating abuse.
- This phenomenon often occurs in domestic violence situations, where abusers harm pets to emotionally abuse and control family members.

Mechanisms of Entangled Victimization

- Abusers use threats to harm pets to silence or coerce children, leveraging the child's emotional bond with the animal.
- Children may witness or be forced to participate in neglect, physical, or sexual abuse of their pets, causing deep trauma.

Implications and Outcomes

- The abuse of pets is a method to terrorize the child, making the animal's suffering a tool for emotional control.
- Despite the trauma, some children develop compassion and caring attitudes toward animals as they grow into adulthood.
