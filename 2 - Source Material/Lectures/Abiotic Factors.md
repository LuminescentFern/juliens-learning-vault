# Abiotic Factors

This lecture explains why certain plants grow in specific ecosystems, focusing on the abiotic factors that influence plant distribution.

Abiotic Factors Influencing Plant Growth

- Sunlight is essential for photosynthesis, which allows plants to convert carbon dioxide and sunlight into energy and grow.
- Water availability and temperature also affect plant survival; for example, oak trees need ample water and can tolerate some shade, while many grasses require direct sunlight.

Regional Differences in Ecosystems

- Different regions, like Southeast Texas and West Texas, have distinct ecosystems due to variations in rainfall, temperature, and soil type.
- Soil composition, such as clay-rich soils in Houston versus sandy or rocky soils in West Texas, influences which plants can thrive.

Local Scale Influences: Fire and Vegetation

- Fire plays a crucial role in determining whether an area becomes a forest or prairie.
- Grasses are adapted to survive wildfires by regrowing from roots, while oak trees are less fire-tolerant, so frequent fires favor prairie ecosystems over forests.
