# Wildlife tourism

This lecture explains the concept of wildlife tourism, focusing on intentional encounters with wildlife as a travel goal, and explores the sociological motivations behind such experiences.

Definition and Types of Wildlife Tourism

- Wildlife tourism involves purposeful travel to see animals, distinct from incidental wildlife sightings during other trips.
- It includes consumptive activities like hunting and non-consumptive ones like photographing or watching animals, with emphasis here on non-consumptive tourism.

Role of Virtual Capital in Tourism Experiences

- Tourism involves an ideological reframing shaped by cultural knowledge and expectations, called virtual capital.
- Visitors’ experiences, including wildlife encounters, are influenced by prior knowledge from media, education, and culture, shaping their expectations and perceptions.

Theories Explaining Motivation for Wildlife Tourism

- The Biophilia Hypothesis suggests humans have an innate need to connect with nature, which benefits mental and physical well-being.
- Attention Restoration Theory explains that wildlife encounters provide effortless fascination that restores mental focus fatigued by daily tasks requiring direct attention.

Additional Considerations

- These explanations focus on human benefits and do not address impacts on wildlife.
- Wildlife tourism is a privilege for those who can afford it, but simple nature experiences like birdwatching are accessible to all and offer similar restorative benefits.
