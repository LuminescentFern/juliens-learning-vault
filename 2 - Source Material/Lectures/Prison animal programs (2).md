# Prison animal programs (2)

This course item explains the structure, operation, and benefits of prison animal programs, particularly those involving dog training.

Prison Animal Program Structure

- These programs typically involve inmates training dogs from shelters or breeders for obedience, socialization, or specialized service tasks.
- Participation is selective, requiring good behavior and no history of animal abuse, with inmates receiving specialized training and living with the dogs.

Benefits for Inmates

- Inmates gain vocational skills useful for employment after release, including certification opportunities and connections to organizations.
- The programs support identity transformation (relabeling), helping inmates develop responsibility, self-esteem, and a new non-criminal identity.

Therapeutic and Broader Benefits

- Interaction with dogs provides therapeutic effects such as reduced loneliness, lower tension, and improved emotional well-being for inmates and staff.
- The programs also benefit dogs by providing training and adoption opportunities, support partner organizations, and help the wider community by increasing service dog availability and shelter capacity.
