# Factors that limit population growth

This course content explains how population sizes change over time and the factors that influence these changes, using examples like elephants in Kruger National Park and Canadian lynx.

Population Growth and Limiting Factors

- Elephant populations initially grew exponentially in Kruger National Park due to protection from poaching, despite their slow reproductive cycle.
- Population growth is limited by density-dependent factors such as competition for resources, waste accumulation, and disease spread, which intensify as population density increases.

Density-Independent Factors and Carrying Capacity

- Density-independent factors like natural disasters, severe weather, and pollution can reduce population size regardless of density.
- Populations tend to stabilize around a carrying capacity, the maximum sustainable population size determined by species needs and environmental resources.

Population Fluctuations and Predator-Prey Dynamics

- Populations often overshoot carrying capacity, leading to increased mortality and subsequent population decline, causing oscillations around the carrying capacity.
- The Canadian lynx and snowshoe hare populations demonstrate predator-prey cycles, where each species' population size influences the other's in a continuous feedback loop.
