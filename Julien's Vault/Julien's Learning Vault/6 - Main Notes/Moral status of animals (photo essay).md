**Image 1:** This picture displays a block of goat butter resting on top of my oven. The first thing I noticed about the goat butter in my fridge was the packaging. The imagery would make one believe that the goats are living a happy life frolicking in fields when in reality they are most likely living inside in close quarters. I've realized since exploring the animal-industrial complex that a lot of the advertising and packaging we see is very misleading. The goats will likely live in their own waste, with very little room to move. Every aspect of their life is controlled including what they eat, their range of movement, and their environment. Their lives are far from happy, and many of these animals are suffering deeply. This isn't something I would have paid attention to before taking this course.

**Image 2:** This picture displays an egg in my hand, which I took out of my fridge. This egg came from a local farm. The egg in this photo likely came from cramped environments where the hens are lined up and trapped in cages with slots for feeding placed in front of them. The hens are bred for the maximum egg laying efficiency, and as a result will likely suffer health issues because of it. The feed provided to the chickens are loaded with antibiotics that prevent infection that could reduce the quality of their eggs. One new thing I've learned is how antibiotics are fed to the chickens, before I thought they were injected into them. I've also learned how in some egg farms where the hens are able to interact with each other, They are debeaked to prevent fights causing harm.
