# Animals and society specialization reflection


How has this course shaped your views on animals in society?

How do you anticipate using what you have learned in your everyday life?

What topics would you like to learn more about? 

How do you plan to gain this additional knowledge?

------

This course has taught me a lot that I've found quite interesting. For example, the way a dog can liven up a room and make people more social, some part of me certainly noticed this before but I never really thought about it deeply until doing this course. 

Module 1 has certainly made me more keen to get a pet in the future. Module 2 has changed how I interact with and look at animals, along with that it has given me a look inside their brains that I've rarely stopped to consider before. Module 3 has given me multiple perspectives of animal rights along with the mentalities behind them, it has given me a better idea of where I stand on the animal rights and animal welfare topic, and I found it interesting to learn about some of the roots of animal rights and animal welfare. 

Although I cannot use what I've learned here in much of my daily life, it still does affect a lot of it. My perspectives on animals have been substantially changed in ways I've noticed in my thinking, and I will never interact or view them the same. Previous experiences with animals have been made much clearer and  it's fascinating when I look back on them. 

I personally love learning about the interesting little stories that are important to the courses (Such as how pounds & animal shelters came to be, the symbolism on violent animal sports, etc.) Along with this, I've loved learning about the effects animals have on humans, the effects humans have on animals, and I've enjoyed learning about the minds and behaviors of animals. 

I plan to continue learning about animals wherever I get the opportunity. Whether it be sitting on a bench watching squirrels move among the trees and interacting with each other, listening to stories of experiences others have had with animals, or reading books about topics with animals that I find interesting. I do not wish to pursue a career in animal welfare or animal rights at this time, however, these three courses have taught me many valuable lessons that I believe everyone should know.
