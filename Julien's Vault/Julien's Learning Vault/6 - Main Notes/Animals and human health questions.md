# Animals and human health questions


After reading the following [study on animals and human health](https://docs.lib.purdue.edu/paij/vol2/iss1/5/ "study on animals and human health"), answer the four questions below on the forum: 

Papotto, Elisa Maria Concetta and Oliva, Jessica Lee. 2019. "Paws for Thought: The Importance of Dogs in a Seniors Social Intervention." _People and Animals: The International Journal of Research and Practice_ 2(1).

**Discussion questions**

1. In your own words, how would you describe the purpose of the study?
    
2. What was the “gap” in the research that the authors were trying to fill by doing their study?
    
3. Do the conclusions made (about the results) make sense to you? 
    
4. Are the conclusions too broad or too narrow based on what was actually done in the study?

-----

The authors of the study identified a gap between evidence that dogs and AAI can improve social connection, positive emotions and well-being in senior citizens and the little focus on community based interventions for reducing loneliness later in life. 

However, Any conclusion that AAI solves loneliness, improve health for all older adults, or should be adopted as a primary intervention would be too broad based on the evidence in the study. Although the study does show potential benefits and associations, it also notes important limitations such as senior citizens reduced capacity to care for pets. It would make more sense to say that AAI may be helpful to some seniors as a complementary strategy for supporting well-being, and that further research and evaluation would be helpful.
