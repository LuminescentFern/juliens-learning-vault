# Animal shelter dilemma

Decisions about animal welfare often involve dilemmas. The vignettes below describe hypothetical dilemmas in animal sheltering. In each case, discuss how you would deal with the situation. 

As part of your job at an animal shelter, you evaluate whether animals should be made available for adoption or euthanized. One day, a dog you evaluate has a history of aggression to other animals. He has fought with several other dogs, both on and off leash, and once fatally injured a cat. If you were assessing this animal for adoption, what would you do?

In your role as adoption counselor at an animal shelter, you meet with a  client who has come to adopt a cat. While completing the paperwork, you learn that she already has four cats at home. What would you do?

------

Dilemma 1: Considering the dog seems to be fine with humans, it should receive proper training to become less aggressive towards other animals. The dog is still far from a "lost cause" and should still be put up for adoption. 

Dilemma 2: The amount of cats one should be allowed to own is very subjective. However, I think it depends on a few things: The size of the house, the number of caretakers, the clients mental state and age, and the clients financial situation. Lets assume all of these are on the optimal side, then 5 cats is a fair number of cats to own. However if some of these aren't on the optimal side, than I do not believe the woman should be allowed to own any more cats, and 4 is already a lot.
