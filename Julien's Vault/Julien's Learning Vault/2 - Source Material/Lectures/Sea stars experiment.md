# Sea stars experiment

This course content explains a key ecological experiment that revealed the important role of certain species in maintaining ecosystem diversity.

Ecological Research Approaches

- In the 1960s, ecology evolved to include more mathematical models and experimental methods beyond just observation.
- Experiments allow ecologists to test hypotheses about species roles and ecosystem processes.

Paine's Sea Star Experiment

- Ecologist Bob Paine removed Purple Sea Stars, a top predator, from rocky intertidal zones in Washington State to observe effects.
- Removing sea stars led to a drastic reduction in species diversity, with mussels dominating the ecosystem.

Keystone Species Concept

- Paine coined "keystone species" to describe species like sea stars that have a disproportionately large effect on their ecosystems.
- Predators like sea stars maintain diversity by controlling dominant prey species, preventing ecosystem collapse.
