# Barnacles

This course content explains Joseph Connell's 1961 ecological study on barnacles in the intertidal zone, focusing on how competition and environmental factors influence species distribution.

Connell's Observations and Experiments

- Two barnacle species occupy different vertical zones on the rocky shore: one higher near the high tide line (families) and one lower (semi balinese).
- Connell experimentally removed one species at a time to observe changes in distribution and competition effects.

Competition and Habitat Occupation

- Removing the lower-zone barnacle (semi balinese) allowed the higher-zone barnacle (families) to spread downward, indicating families can live lower but is usually outcompeted.
- Removing the higher-zone barnacle (families) did not lead to semi balinese moving upward, suggesting other factors limit its range.

Environmental and Biotic Factors

- The higher zone is exposed to air longer, and semi balinese is less tolerant of drying out, explaining its absence there.
- Both physiological tolerance to abiotic factors (like desiccation) and biotic competition shape where each barnacle species lives in the intertidal zone.
