# The animal-industrial complex (2)


This lecture focuses on the environmental and social consequences of industrialized animal agriculture, particularly large-scale concentrated animal feeding operations (CFOs).

Environmental Impact of Industrial Animal Agriculture

- CFOs house large numbers of animals, such as hogs, in confined sheds with waste stored in deep pits and outdoor lagoons, which can overflow during floods, causing pollution and dead zones in water bodies.
- Livestock production uses about 30% of the Earth's ice-free land, including grazing and feed crop production, leading to deforestation, soil degradation, and erosion, especially in regions like the Amazon.

Water Use and Pollution

- Animal agriculture accounts for over 8% of global water use, mainly for irrigating feed crops.
- It contributes significantly to water pollution through runoff of fertilizers, pesticides, animal waste, antibiotics, and hormones, harming aquatic ecosystems and human health.

Social Effects of Animal Industrial Complex

- The relocation of slaughterhouses to rural areas has brought social challenges, including housing shortages, strain on local resources, increased crime, and health issues among workers, who are often young immigrant males.
- These social costs highlight the broader community impact beyond just economic benefits.

Overall, the lecture emphasizes understanding the wide-ranging impacts of current industrial animal farming practices to consider alternatives.
