# Animals in laboratories (1)


This lecture explains the various ways animals are used in research, the types of animals involved, and their sources.

Uses of Animals in Research

- Fundamental research studies basic biological, physiological, or behavioral processes, using the largest number of animals.
- Biomedical research tests new therapies and treatments for humans or animals, focusing on prevention and treatment.

Types of Animal Research and Testing

- Genetic engineering creates genetically modified animals for disease study or prevention, including self-limiting mosquitoes to control disease spread.
- Product testing involves animals for safety and toxicity checks of consumer goods, with some regions banning animal-tested cosmetics.

Sources and Numbers of Research Animals

- About 780,000 animals were reported used in U.S. research in 2018, excluding purpose-bred rodents, birds, and fish, which could raise the total to 11-23 million.
- Animals come from licensed commercial dealers: Class A dealers breed animals for sale, while Class B dealers buy from random sources; NIH-funded research requires Class A sources.
