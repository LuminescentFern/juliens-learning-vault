# The Sociozoologic Scale

This course content explains how humans culturally and socially categorize animals, influencing how we perceive and treat them.

The Sociozoologic Scale

- Animals are ranked on a hierarchy of value based on human cultural meanings, not biology.
- Companion animals like dogs and cats are placed high on the scale, while pests like cockroaches and rattlesnakes are placed low.

Cultural Meanings and Moral Value

- Animals are assigned moral and social value through cultural definitions, such as pets being valued more than pests.
- These meanings affect how society justifies treatment of different species, including legal and ethical considerations.

Implications for Human-Animal Relationships

- Animals with high moral status receive more concern for their welfare and protection.
- Animals considered pests or threats are often killed or controlled without regard for their well-being, reflecting their low status on the sociozoologic scale.
