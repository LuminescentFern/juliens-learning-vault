# Doing gender with pets

This lecture explores how gender perceptions influence the way people relate to dogs and cats, reflecting broader social ideas about masculinity and femininity.

Gender Associations with Dogs and Cats

- Dogs are culturally associated with masculinity and men, while cats are linked to femininity and women, regardless of the animals' actual sex.
- Language and popular culture reinforce these associations, with terms like "man's best friend" for dogs and "crazy cat lady" for women.

Gender Display and Pet Ownership

- People "do gender" through their relationships with pets, using them to express or reinforce their gender identity.
- Men often emphasize active roles with dogs (e.g., hunting, exercise), while women highlight affection and nurturing aspects.

Social Perceptions and Cultural Practices

- Men owning cats may face challenges to their masculinity, as shown in historical and contemporary studies of how men and women interact with cats.
- These gendered pet ownership patterns reflect and reproduce societal stereotypes about gender roles and behaviors.
