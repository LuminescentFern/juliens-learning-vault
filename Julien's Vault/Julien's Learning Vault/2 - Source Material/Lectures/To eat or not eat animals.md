# To eat or not eat animals

This lecture addresses the moral and practical questions surrounding the consumption of animals and animal products.

Moral Perspectives on Eating Animals

- Many people eat meat and animal products despite moral concerns about killing animals.
- Some justify eating certain animals (like cows and chickens) but not others (like dogs and cats) by perceiving differences in intelligence or capacity to suffer.

Health and Environmental Considerations

- Some avoid meat for health reasons or due to concerns about animal agriculture's impact on climate change.
- These concerns focus more on the effects of animal farming rather than the act of killing animals.

Ethical Solutions and Challenges

- Avoiding all animal products is a clear solution for those opposed to killing animals.
- Others seek meat from animals raised and slaughtered humanely to reduce suffering, though this is often impractical or unaffordable for most people.

Social and Psychological Ambiguity

- People experience cognitive dissonance when continuing to eat meat despite knowing about animal suffering.
- Cultural and social meanings assigned to different animals influence which are considered acceptable to eat, and these meanings can change over time.
