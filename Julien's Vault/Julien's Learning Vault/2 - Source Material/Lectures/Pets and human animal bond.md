# Pets and human animal bond

This lecture explores the concept of pets, their historical and cultural significance, and the human-animal bond.

Definition and Types of Pets

- Pets are favorite animals given individual names, allowed in human homes, and not eaten; any species can be a pet, including common ones like dogs and cats, as well as exotic pets like reptiles and rodents.
- Some animals, such as horses or favored livestock, can be considered both pets and commodities, reflecting complex human-animal relationships.

Historical Evidence of Human-Animal Relationships

- Archaeological findings show intentional burial of dogs and cats, indicating ancient companionship dating back thousands of years.
- Notable discoveries include a 14,000-year-old burial of a dog with humans in Germany and a 9,500-year-old burial of a human with a cat in Cyprus, suggesting early special relationships.

The Human-Animal Bond Today

- Historically, dogs and cats served functional roles like hunting and guarding, but since the 19th century, their role has shifted primarily to companionship.
- The human-animal bond involves emotional and physical closeness, supported by veterinary advances that allow healthy cohabitation, and is defined by the American Veterinary Medical Association as a mutually beneficial relationship essential to the well-being of both humans and animals.
