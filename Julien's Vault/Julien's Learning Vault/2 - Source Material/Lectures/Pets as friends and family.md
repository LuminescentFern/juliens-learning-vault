# Pets as friends and family

This lecture explores the meaningful relationships between humans and their pets, focusing on how pets are often regarded as friends or family members rather than mere property.

Friendship with Pets

- Friendship involves shared enjoyable activities and mutual interest, such as spending time together and making eye contact, which dogs excel at and cats engage with differently.
- Pets demonstrate the ability to share intentions and feelings with humans, recognizing them as conscious partners in interaction.

Pets as Family Members

- Pets influence household routines and spaces, often gaining access to areas traditionally reserved for humans and participating in family rituals.
- Owners may refer to themselves as pet parents and include pets in family photos, highlighting the kinship and unique individuality of each pet.

Conditional Status and Challenges

- Pets' status as family members depends on their behavior and compliance with human-established rules, with issues like aggression or shedding potentially straining relationships.
- Changes in life circumstances can make pet care burdensome, leading to many pets ending up in shelters despite their previous family status.
