# How prey avoid predators

The course content explains how prey species have evolved various strategies to avoid being eaten by predators, highlighting the dynamic interaction between predators and prey.

Physical and Behavioral Defenses

- Prey use physical defenses like hard shells or spines to deter predators, as seen in spiny walking sticks.
- Camouflage and slow movement help prey avoid detection by blending into their environment and minimizing movement that predators notice.

Warning Coloration and Aposematism

- Some prey species use bright, contrasting colors to warn predators they are dangerous or toxic, a strategy called aposematism.
- Poisonous species like certain butterflies have unpleasant tastes that teach predators to avoid them, benefiting both the individual and others with similar patterns.

Mimicry Types

- Müllerian mimicry occurs when two harmful species share similar warning colors to reinforce predator avoidance.
- Batesian mimicry happens when a harmless species mimics a dangerous one’s coloration to gain protection from predators.
