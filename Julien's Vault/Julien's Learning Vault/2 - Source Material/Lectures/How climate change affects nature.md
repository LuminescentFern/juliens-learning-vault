# How climate change affects nature

The course content explains how climate change affects species by forcing them to either adapt, move, or face extinction.

Species Adaptation and Movement

- Species have specific temperature ranges they can tolerate; as temperatures rise, they must evolve or relocate.
- Evolution is often too slow to keep pace with rapid climate change, so many species move to cooler areas.

Examples of Species Shifts

- Studies of butterflies in Europe show 65% have shifted their ranges northward to maintain suitable climates.
- Mountain plants have moved upward in elevation, and corals are dying due to warming seas causing coral bleaching.

Limits and Consequences

- Species must move about one kilometer per year to keep up with climate change, but space is limited.
- Species at mountain tops, far northern regions, or low-lying islands face high risks of extinction if they cannot move or adapt.
