# Consider the lobster


David Foster Wallace’s _Consider the Lobster_ begins as a report on the Maine Lobster Festival but develops into an exploration of the ethics of eating lobsters. Wallace examines how lobsters are caught, prepared, and commonly boiled alive, raising questions about whether they can feel pain and suffer. He discusses scientific evidence about lobster nervous systems and behaviour while highlighting the discomfort many people feel when considering the treatment of animals used for food. Rather than arguing for or against eating lobster, Wallace encourages readers to think critically about the moral implications of causing potential suffering for human pleasure and to reflect on the ethical responsibilities involved in food consumption.
