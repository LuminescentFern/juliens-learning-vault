# Animals, Self and Society


This course explores the complex relationships between animals and humans within society, focusing on symbolic roles, interactions, and ethical considerations.

Thinking with Animals

- Examines how animals are symbolically represented and the meanings people assign to them.
- Investigates the implications of these meanings for both animals and humans, including media representations.

Human-Animal Relationships

- Explores the nature of friendships and family-like bonds between humans and animals.
- Discusses the darker aspects of these relationships, such as animal abuse and its connection to domestic violence.

Encounters with Wild Animals

- Focuses on human interactions with wild animals outside domestic and farm settings.
- Considers issues of coexistence, conflict, and the moral status of wild species, including insects
