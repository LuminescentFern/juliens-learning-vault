# Linnaeus' Quest

This lecture focuses on the goal of biology to name and classify all living things on Earth, highlighting the contributions of Carl Linnaeus and the challenges in documenting biodiversity.

Linnaeus and the Binomial Naming System

- Linnaeus developed the binomial system of naming species, using two Latin names: genus and species (e.g., Homo sapiens).
- His system was influenced by the Sami people’s naming traditions during his expedition to Lapland, where he collected and documented many species.

Linnaeus' Ambitions and Challenges

- Linnaeus aimed to catalog all species on Earth by sending students worldwide to collect specimens.
- He often took credit for naming species collected by his students, and his goal was unrealistic due to the vast biodiversity, especially in tropical regions.

Biodiversity Patterns and Ecological Questions

- Species richness declines toward the poles and at higher altitudes, with tropical regions being the most diverse.
- Understanding why some regions have more species than others is a central question in ecology, involving complex factors that ecologists continue to study.
