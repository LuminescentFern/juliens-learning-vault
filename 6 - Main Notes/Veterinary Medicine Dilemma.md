# Veterinary Medicine Dilemma

Decisions about animal welfare often involve dilemmas. The vignettes below describe hypothetical dilemmas in veterinary medicine. In each case, discuss how you would deal with the situation. 

A client brings in a blind, fifteen-year-old dog who belonged to her mother. Other than blindness, which affects both eyes, the dog appears healthy. The client requests euthanasia because her mother has moved into assisted living and cannot keep the dog. The client already has two active dogs, and her home would not be safe for a blind dog, as it is a split-level design. The client has already asked several friends if they would like to take the dog, but nothing has worked out. If you were the veterinarian, what would you do? 

A man who has been your client for a long time brings in a cat and claims that he has ruined the couch by scratching. The client requests that you perform declawing surgery. You recommend scratching posts, but the client objects to the way they look. If you were the veterinarian, what would you do?

------

Dilemma 1: My first thought was putting the dog up for adoption, however, that would be cruel for a 15 year old dog who has lived in the same family all of its life. Perhaps the dog could be trained to avoid the dangerous areas and make its way around them, and if that doesn't work out, euthanasia unfortunately may be the best option. 

Dilemma 2: This one is much simpler, claw caps. Claw caps can be put onto cats claws and will prevent the cat from causing damage via scratching. Along with this it does not affect the cats life much, and is for sure the better option over declawing.
